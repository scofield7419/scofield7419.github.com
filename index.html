Profile

I am currently a Ph.D student at School of Cyber Science and Engineering of Wuhan University. Before that, I received my M.S. degree from Wuhan University in 2018, and B.S. degree from Xidian University in 2016.
My research direction is natural language processing (NLP), with the fundamental goal of building models capable of human-level understanding of natural language. I majorly focus on the structure-aware NLP, including 1) Semantics/Syntax Parsing, 2) Affective Computing, and 3) Information Extraction, etc. During my Ph.D researching phase, I have published more than 20 papers in top-tier NLP/DM/ML conferences and journals, including ACL, AAAI, WWW, IJCAI, EMNLP, COLING, TNNLS, TASLP, etc.

I am apt to construct models with deep learning technique, especially employing the syntax features for downstream tasks. But sometimes I also believe the data-driven methods can be more powerful. As future directions, I will dedicate my efforts to:

  • continuing the structure-aware tasks, but with broader scenario, i.e., integrating syntax/semantic structure into text generation, such as summarization, dialog, translation, multimodal etc;
  • co-modeling the syntactic skeleton and semantic meaning of texts, e.g., by defining graphs;
  • developing explainable NLP;
  • building highly-robust NLP models and applications.
